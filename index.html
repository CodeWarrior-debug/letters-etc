Champions Update

Greetings, Team Champions!

Recently a pastor couple from Ciudad Sandino, Nicaragua came to visit Greenville, SC for a few days to participate in an activity at our church before going to check in with a number of their supporting congregations in various parts of the US. While they were here, we took them out to breakfast one morning. Several of the youth that attend their church in Ciudad Sandino participate in Champions for Jesus, and the pastors remarked on how Champions has come to be a well-known and well respected ministry in the community. It was special to hear those words coming from other spiritual leaders in the community where we are at work.

Here are a few highlights from the last few months that we’d like to share with you:

New Supplies

We were able to finish purchasing needed new supplies for the court and the office thanks to a mission grant from Grace Church in Greenville, SC. Kids now have a place to sit (other than the floor) when hanging out after school or gathering for Bible study! They also have new sports equipment to use during practices and games. Take a look!

New Participants

At the beginning of this year, we had an influx of young participants (under 12). We estimate that more than half of these new participants in Ciudad Sandino (our original and primary location) have stayed with us throughout the year. Not quite two years ago, we started a second Champions Academy in a neighborhood called Los Brasiles, and our program there has also continued to grow: there are about 60 children consistently participating in weekly activities, and the majority of these are plugged in at local churches.

Recent Achievements

In my last letter, I shared about the successes of several of our teams during national championships this year. Shortly following my letter, Luis Pavón, the president of the Nicaraguan national basketball federation (Fenibalón) who thought highly of Champions and was a supportive connection in many ways, unexpectedly suffered a series of strokes and later passed away. In the months that followed, there was a pause in basketball tournaments and events on the national level as the organization worked to identify a new president and regroup. Our Field Manager, Klaimer Duarte, was offered the position but turned it down because he said he prefers his current job! We’re grateful he loves what he does!

Nevertheless, municipal tournaments (all of Ciudad Sandino) continued to take place, and our girls’ U19 team were champions for the third consecutive year. For the boys, our U14 team won first place, and the U15 and U18 teams won second place.

Another exciting development has been to observe several older Champions participants developing leadership qualities as they lead younger teams during practices, games, and Bible studies. These “captains” are also helping manage communication among their peers regarding reminders for practices, games, and special events, visiting their teammates at home to check on them if they miss a practice, etc.

Raising Up Leaders

It is amazing to see how, over the last decade, Champions has developed young leaders who are now being recognized and sought for external leadership opportunities. As previously mentioned, Klaimer was sought to fill the position of president of Nicaragua’s national basketball federation but declined (luckily for us!).

Ismael, who grew up in Champions and now works on staff half-time to provide the same programming for younger individuals, was offered a post as Basketball Coordinator for the mayor’s office of Ciudad Sandino. Ismael also declined because he wanted to continue working in his current role with Champions.

In the end, Alejandro Chávez Picado was hired for the position with the office of the mayor; he, too, is a graduate of Champions programs with a desire to give back to his community. What a testimony that young men growing up in and graduating from Champions Academy as well as leaders of this well-respected ministry are so recognized for their character and experience that they are sought for leadership opportunities locally and nationally!

In Their Words

Alejandro Josue Chávez Picado
“When I first came to Champions, I was an undisciplined person. I didn’t like to follow orders, and I always wanted to do what I wanted. Klaimer invited me to train for a U-18 competition, and I was a disaster: I didn’t attend practices, and I was undisciplined and disrespectful. In the end, Klaimer decided not to take me to the competition.

“After that, there came another tournament. I did everything possible in order to be able to go. I started to change my life, and beginning in that moment I began to be more disciplined and to take opportunities more seriously. Champions and the coaches gave me another opportunity, and it was the best thing I could have done.

“For me, Champions is my second family. I think that if I had not gotten involved in Champions, my life would have been completely different and I would have made wrong choices. Now, thanks to the formation that Champions for Jesus gave me in basketball as well as moral and spiritual values and to the references of Klaimer and the coaches, I obtained a job as Director and Coordinator of Basketball for the mayor’s office of Ciudad Sandino.”

William Salazar (father of Ian)
“ I began getting more involved in my son’s games and the activities they do in Champions. As a result, I now have a close relationship with my son. Champions gave me an opportunity as a father to get to know my son better. I am proud of the person that my son is becoming thanks to Champions.”

For context on William's quote, Ian is a student in Champions Academy. He has been participating in the Academy for almost four years. Now, as one of the team captains and a volunteer in Champions, Ian is an example for younger children.

In one of the national tournaments where we emerged victorious, Ian’s father, William was very proud of his son and told us that he was very thankful that Champions had given his son this opportunity. He said that since Ian began participating in Champions, things began to change in his son’s life.
